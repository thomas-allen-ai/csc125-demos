The 8-Puzzle as a Search Problem

The 8-puzzle isn't just a game—it's a classic example of a search problem. Understanding how to solve it systematically is the foundation of classical AI.

The Vocabulary of Search

State
A specific arrangement of the tiles. The puzzle has 181,440 possible states.
Action
A valid move: sliding a tile into the empty space. From any state, you have 2–4 possible actions.
Goal State
Tiles arranged 1–8 in order, with the empty space in the bottom-right corner.
Search Space
All possible states connected by valid moves. You're searching this space for a path from your starting state to the goal.

What the Numbers Mean

Depth How many moves you've made from the start. This is how "deep" you are in the search tree.
Distance Manhattan distance—a heuristic that estimates how far you are from solved. For each tile, count how many squares it needs to move (horizontally + vertically) to reach its goal position. Add them all up. Lower is better. Zero means solved.
States Seen How many different arrangements you've visited. This shows the "cost" of your exploration.

Backtracking

When you hit a dead end—no good moves available—you backtrack: undo moves until you find a point where you can try something different.

This is exactly how a depth-first search algorithm works. You're doing it by hand.

The Undo button lets you backtrack. The Path history shows your current path so you can see where you've been.

Heuristic Hints

Toggle "Show heuristic hints" to see a number on each movable tile:

  • -2 means this move decreases the Manhattan distance by 2—you're getting closer to solved
  • +1 means this move increases the distance—you're moving away from the goal
  • 0 means no change

A greedy strategy always picks the most negative number. This often works, but sometimes you have to "go uphill" temporarily to find the solution. That's why greedy search isn't always optimal.

  • Set it to 10. Much harder! Watch your "States Seen" grow.

  • Try solving without undo. How does that change your approach?
